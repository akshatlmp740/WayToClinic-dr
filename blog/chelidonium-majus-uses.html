What is Chelidonium Majus?

Chelidonium majus, commonly known as Greater Celandine, is a plant recognized for its distinctive yellow-orange sap and has been traditionally used for various medicinal purposes. This plant is notable for its application in treating conditions related to the liver, gallbladder, and digestive system. Despite its historical usage, including the removal of warts using its toxic sap, modern medical evidence supporting these benefits is lacking, and its use can be unsafe.

Chelidonium Majus is a biennial plant with angular stems, hairy leaves, and small yellow flowers. It was brought from Europe to the United States as an herbal remedy and is now found in gardens, woodlands, and roadsides. Though it has been used in homeopathy for liver-related ailments, it’s important to be cautious due to its potential hepatotoxic effects.

Chelidonium Majus Chemical Constituents

Chelidonium Majus, or greater celandine, is known for its extensive medicinal benefits, which are primarily due to its rich chemical makeup. Below, we explore some of the active compounds found in this plant and their significant health effects.

  • Chelerythrine: Chelerythrine is a potent compound in Chelidonium Majus. It has strong antibacterial properties, effectively fighting against bacteria like Streptococcus mutans, which causes tooth decay. Additionally, it exhibits antifungal activity, inhibiting the growth of fungi such as Trichophyton and Aspergillus species.
  • Sanguinarine: Sanguinarine is another powerful alkaloid found in Chelidonium Majus. It shows significant antibacterial effects against pathogens like Staphylococcus aureus and Escherichia coli. Sanguinarine also has antiviral properties, helping inhibit the growth of viruses like herpes simplex and HIV.
  • Chelidonine: Chelidonine is known for its diverse medicinal properties. It has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, reducing the production of inflammatory mediators. Additionally, it exhibits hepatoprotective properties, protecting the liver from damage and promoting its health.
  • Berberine: Berberine is a well-known compound in Chelidonium Majus with strong antiviral properties. It helps inhibit the activity of HIV-I reverse transcriptase enzyme, which is crucial in the virus’s replication process. Berberine also exhibits anticancer properties by inducing apoptosis in cancer cells.
  • 8-Hydroxy Dihydro Sanguinarine and 8-Hydroxy Dihydro Chelerythrine: These compounds have been identified for their strong antibacterial and antifungal effects. They are particularly effective against drug-resistant strains of bacteria and fungi, making them valuable in combating infections that are difficult to treat with conventional antibiotics.
  • Chelidonic Acid: Chelidonic acid, another important constituent, has shown promise in reducing inflammation in conditions like ulcerative colitis. It works by inhibiting the production of inflammatory molecules such as IL-6 and TNF-α, thus helping in the management of inflammatory diseases.
  • Alkaloids and Polyphenols: The various alkaloids and polyphenols in Chelidonium Majus contribute to its antioxidant properties. These compounds help scavenge free radicals, protecting cells from oxidative damage. This antioxidant activity is crucial for maintaining overall health and preventing chronic diseases.

Chelidonium Majus Preparation

Chelidonium Majus has been cherished in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties. The process of preparing Chelidonium Majus, especially for homeopathic use, is both meticulous and fascinating. This guide explains how to prepare ultra-high diluted Chelidonium Majus, following the principles established by Dr. Samuel Hahnemann;

  • Collecting and Processing the Plant: The preparation begins with the collection of the entire Chelidonium Majus plant. Once harvested, the plant is dried and ground into a coarse powder. This initial step ensures that all the active compounds are accessible for extraction.
  • Creating the Ethanol Extract: To generate the ethanol extract, a mixture of purified water and ethanol is used. Specifically, 100 grams of the powdered Chelidonium Majus is combined with 567 milliliters of purified water and 468 milliliters of ethanol. This combination produces a total of 1000 milliliters of the ethanol extract. The mixture is carefully blended to ensure thorough extraction of the plant’s beneficial compounds.
  • Ultra-High Dilution Through Succussion: The next step involves preparing ultra-high dilution, a process known as potentization. Potentization enhances the medicinal properties of the extract through a series of dilutions and vigorous shaking, known as succussion. Here’s how it’s done:
  • Initial Dilution: One part of the crude ethanol extract is mixed with nine parts of strong alcohol.
    Succussion: This mixture is then subjected to mechanical shaking, known as succussion. This involves forcefully striking the container against a firm surface or using a potentizer machine to generate kinetic energy.
  • Repeating the Process: This dilution and succussion process is repeated multiple times. Each cycle increases the potency of the preparation. For example, the first dilution (1X) is prepared by mixing one part of the original extract with nine parts of alcohol, succussing the mixture. This process continues through successive dilutions to achieve the desired potency, such as 2X, 3X, and so on.
  • Importance of Potentization: Potentization is crucial because it not only dilutes the original substance but also activates its healing properties. The kinetic energy introduced during succussion enhances the medicinal potential of Chelidonium Majus, making it highly effective in ultra-high dilutions.

Chelidonium Majus Mother Tincture

Chelidonium Majus is a plant from the Papaveraceae family that is used in homeopathy and herbal medicine. The mother tincture of Chelidonium Majus is made using the whole plant, which contains several alkaloids. This guide explains how to prepare the mother tincture in a simple, step-by-step manner;

  • Collecting and Preparing the Plant: First, gather the entire Chelidonium Majus plant. Clean the plant thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris. After cleaning, chop the fresh root into small pieces. Crush these pieces into a fine pulp. This step is important to extract the plant’s juices effectively.
  • Extracting the Juice: Place the crushed root pulp in a piece of clean, new linen cloth. Press the fabric to squeeze out the juice from the pulp. This juice contains the essential compounds needed for the tincture.
  • Mixing with Alcohol: Take the extracted juice and combine it with an equal amount of alcohol. This alcohol helps preserve the active ingredients of the plant. Pour this mixture into a well-sealed bottle.
  • Infusing the Mixture: Store the bottle in a dark, cool place for eight days. Shake the bottle every day to ensure the mixture is well-agitated. This process helps to combine the plant juice with the alcohol thoroughly.
  • Filtering the Tincture: After eight days, filter the mixture to remove any solid plant parts. The resulting liquid is the mother tincture. It will have a brownish-orange color and a bitter taste, and it might smell somewhat like beeswax.
  • Storing the Tincture: Store the filtered tincture in a dark, cool place. This helps maintain its potency and effectiveness over time. Use the tincture as needed, either by itself or diluted with water or another liquid.

Who should avoid Chelidonium Majus?

Chelidonium Majus is a useful homeopathic remedy, but some people should avoid it.

  • Pregnant and breastfeeding women should not take it. There is not enough information to know if it is safe for them.
  • People with autoimmune diseases like multiple sclerosis (MS), lupus, and rheumatoid arthritis should also avoid Chelidonium Majus. This remedy can make the immune system more active, which might worsen their symptoms.
  • Anyone with a blocked bile duct should avoid Chelidonium Majus. This remedy can increase bile flow, which might worsen their condition.
  • People with liver diseases, such as hepatitis, should avoid Chelidonium Majus. There is some evidence that it can cause hepatitis or make liver disease worse.
