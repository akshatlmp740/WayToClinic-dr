Hyoscyamus Niger – Uses, Dosage, Side Effects, Composition And More

By Dheeraj Sharma August 31, 2024 Medicine

What Is Hyoscyamus Niger?

Hyoscyamus niger is also commonly known as henbane, black henbane, and stinking nightshade. It is a poisonous plant that belongs to the tribe Hyoscyameae of the nightshade family Solanaceae. Hyoscyamus niger is native to temperate Europe and Siberia and naturalized in Great Britain and Ireland.

Hyoscyamus niger is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat bone ailments, rheumatism, toothache, asthma, cough, nervous disorders, and stomach pain. It may also be used as an analgesic, sedative, and narcotic, depending on multiple factors.

It is a biennial herb that reaches an average height of 80 cm and grows in rocky and arid areas. Hyoscyamus niger has oval leaves, and the color of its flowers varies from yellow to dull green with purple veins.

Hyoscyamus Niger Chemical Constituents

Hyoscyamus niger, or henbane, has several important chemical constituents which help in the functioning of the plant and helps in treating several problems. Tropane alkaloids consist of hyoscyamine, scopolamine, and atropine.

Hyoscyamine helps in reducing vomiting from motion sickness. Scopolamine, better known as hyoscine, is known for its strong hallucinogenic effects and is used in some psychoactive drugs. Atropine is a racemic mixture of D-hyoscyamine and L-hyoscyamine, and its pharmacological effects are due to binding to muscarinic acetylcholine receptors.

Hyoscyamus niger also contains various other compounds, such as flavonoids, coumarins, sterols, tannins, and terpenes. A crude extract of hyoscyamus niger has been found to have hypotensive, cardiosuppressant, and vasodilator activities.

Hyoscyamus Niger Preparation

Hyoscyamus niger can be prepared in several ways, which depends on the amount being prepared and the disease that is to be treated by it, as it is commonly used in herbal medicine.

  • Infusion: Steep dry the hyoscyamus niger leaves in hot water for about 10-15 minutes and then strain and are ready for consumption.
  • Tincture: Soak the dried hyoscyamus niger leaves in alcohol (usually ethanol) for several weeks and then strain the left-out liquid. Use drops of the formed tincture and dilute it in water.
  • Powder: The dried leaves of hyoscyamus niger are ground and made into a fine powder, which can be further stored in capsules or added to other preparations.
  • Extract: By using hyoscyamus niger, prepare a concentrated liquid extract by soaking it in a solvent like alcohol or glycerin.

Who Should Avoid Hyoscyamus Niger?

Hyoscyamus niger is a type of poisonous plant. Hence it should be consumed under proper guidance and dosage. Also, in some conditions, certain people might observe certain complications and side effects.

  • Children and Infants: There has not been much research on the effect of the usage of hyoscyamus niger on children. Hence, it is advised not to use it frequently.
  • Pregnant and Breastfeeding Women: The alkaloids present in hyoscyamus niger can cross the placenta and may be harmful to the developing fetus. Also, it can be transferred to the baby through breast milk; hence, it is mostly avoided.
  • Elderly Individuals: Old-aged people are more prone to the risks caused by hyoscyamus niger; hence they should not consume it.
  • Individuals with Certain Medical Conditions: Certain people suffering from specific medical conditions like glaucoma, myasthenia gravis, enlarged prostate (benign prostatic hyperplasia), gastrointestinal blockages, and certain heart rhythm disorders should avoid the use of hyoscyamus niger.
