What is Ginkgo Biloba?

Ginkgo Biloba is an ancient tree species revered for centuries for its medicinal properties. Also known as the ‘maidenhair tree,’ it is prominent in traditional medicine and homeopathic practices.

Ginkgo Biloba is unique because it is one of the oldest living tree species, stretching back millions of years. It is native to China and is widely cultivated in many parts of the world. The tree is easily recognizable by its fan-shaped leaves, which turn a brilliant golden yellow in the fall.

The leaves and seeds of the Ginkgo Biloba tree are the primary sources of its medicinal properties. In traditional medicine, Ginkgo Biloba has been used to treat various conditions, including asthma, bronchitis, and circulatory problems. It is also believed to improve memory and cognitive function. It is often used to support brain health and prevent age-related decline.

Ginkgo Biloba Chemical Constituents

Ginkgo Biloba’s therapeutic efficacy is attributed to the presence of several active compounds, including:

  • Ginkgolides: These unique compounds are found only in Ginkgo Biloba. It has potent anti-inflammatory properties.

  • Proanthocyanidins: These antioxidants protect cells from damage caused by free radicals and inflammation. Ginkgo Biloba’s neuroprotective effects occur because of these.

Who Should Avoid Ginkgo Biloba?

Ginkgo Biloba is generally safe for most people, but specific individuals should exercise caution or avoid its use:

  • People with bleeding disorders: If you have a bleeding disorder or take blood thinners, avoid Ginkgo Biloba. It can increase the risk of bleeding.
  • People with epilepsy: Talk to your doctor before using Ginkgo Biloba if you have epilepsy. It may interact with anticonvulsant medications and increase the risk of seizures.
  • People with liver or kidney disease: Consult your doctor before taking Ginkgo Biloba if you have liver or kidney problems. It may be processed differently in people with these conditions.
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women: Avoid Ginkgo Biloba if you are pregnant or breastfeeding. It may not be safe for you or your baby.

What are the Side Effects and Risks of Using Ginkgo Biloba?

In general, Ginkgo Biloba is well-tolerated, but some people may experience side effects, such as:

  • Upset stomach: Ginkgo Biloba can cause nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea in some people.
  • Headaches: Ginkgo Biloba may cause headaches in some people, especially when taken in high doses.
  • Skin reactions: Ginkgo Biloba may cause skin rashes or itching in some people.

Ginkgo Biloba can interact with certain medications, so it’s important to talk to your doctor before taking it if you are on any medications.
