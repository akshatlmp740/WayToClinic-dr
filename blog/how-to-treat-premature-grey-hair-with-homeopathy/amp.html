What is Premature Grey Hair?

Premature grey hair means that an individual developed one or more grey or white hair before the normally expected age. This most often takes place when melanocytes situated in the hair follicles are unable to create melanin, which makes up color. Greying is associated with aging, but premature greying occurs at a younger age, even before thirty years.

There are definitely some hereditary links, but stress, nutrition, smoking, and even pollution can all also cause this condition. There are specific diseases like anemia in vitamin B12, thyroid disease, and autoimmune diseases through which one may be disabled at an early stage of grey hair. While this condition is mainly hereditary, it is often possible to slow the progression of it with proper diet, etc.

Causes of Premature Grey Hair

Here are the main causes:

  • Genetics: Genetics are considered the primary reason that contributes to premature greying. If your parents or grandparents went grey early in their lives, the likelihood of going grey also runs in your genes.
  • Vitamin Deficiencies: Lack of such nutrients as B12, iron, copper, and folate affects the formation of melanin, resulting in early grey hair.
  • Stress: Stress is thought to alter the rate at which melanocytes- pigment synthesis cells of hair follicles get damaged or have a low melanin production rate, which causes premature grey hair formation even though the duration is still an area of research.
  • Hormonal Changes: Early grey hair is also attributed to hormonal changes such as low thyroid levels thus the need to have a balanced diet to ensure this part of the body is functioning well. Other thyroid problems, such as hypothyroidism it is a known fact that this is associated with early-stage greying.
  • Autoimmune Diseases: Pigment-producing cells- melanocytes, are destroyed or damaged in autoimmune diseases like Vitiligo or Alopecia areata, and this prematurely ages hair follicles, resulting in grey hairs.
  • Smoking: Smoking has also been said to cause early greying because it impacts the cells of the body that produce melanin through oxidative stress.
  • Environmental Factors: Pollution, Ultraviolet radiation, and exposure to chemicals all affect the hair and the melanocytes in the skin, and since the melanocytes produce the melanin, which is responsible for hair color, then premature greying occurs.
  • Health Conditions: A condition such as anemia, diabetes, or early menopause puts the health of an individual at risk, hence leading to early grey hair.
  • Aging: Although this premature greying is said to have occurred when hair color is lost at an earlier age than expected, an aging process is also a cause of grey hair. As a person grows older, the pigment-producing cell known as melanocytes lose their ability to produce melanin and thus grow grey hair.
