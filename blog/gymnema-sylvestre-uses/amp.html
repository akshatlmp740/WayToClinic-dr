Gymnema Sylvestre – Uses, Dosage, Side Effects, Composition And More

What Is Gymnema Sylvestre?

Gymnema Sylvestre is a kind of perennial woody vine local to Asia, Africa, and Australia. It has a wide application in Ayurvedic medicine. The common names of gymnema sylvestre is gymnema, Australian cowplant, and Periploca of the woods. The leaves and extracts of Gymnema Sylvestre consist of gymnemic acids, which are the major bioactive constituents.

These interact with taste receptors on the tongue to suppress the taste of sweetness in the body temporarily. The gymnema sylvestre plant is a climber with leaves having soft hairs on the upper surface. The leaves of gymnema sylvestre are elongated and oval. It has a small, yellow, umbelliferous inflorescence that is produced throughout the year.

Gymnema Sylvestre Chemical Constituents

The leaves of gymnema sylvestre consists of chemical constituents such as triterpenoid saponins, flavonols, and gurmarin. The major biologically active plant molecules found are gymnemic acids, a class of triterpenoid saponins, which have the effect of suppressing the taste of sweetness on the tongue from sucrose (sugar), stevia, xylitol, and artificial sweeteners such as aspartame.

The sweet-blocking effect of gymnema sylvestre lasts from 15 to 50 minutes and may even persist for a few hours. Gymnemic acids generally have no long-term effects on taste, and they do not influence bitter, salty, or sour taste sensations.

Gymnema sylvestre leaves consist of triterpene saponins, which belong to the oleanane and dammarene classes. Oleanane saponins are basically gymnemic acids and gymnemasaponins, while dammarene saponins are gymnemasides.

Apart from this, other plant constituents are flavones, anthraquinones, hentri-acontane, pentatriacontane, and -chlorophylls, phytin, resins, d-quercitol, tartaric acid, formic acid, butyric acid, lupeol, -amyrin related glycosides and stigmasterol. Leaves of this species yield acidic glycosides and anthraquinones and their derivatives.

Gymnema Sylvestre Preparation

Gymnema sylvestre, also known as gurmar in Hindi, can be prepared in several ways depending on the result desired.

  • Leaves: To harvest the leaves and flowers, hands are used; a knife or a sickle can also be helpful. Spread the leaves out thinly in the shade for 78 days to dry, ensuring good airflow and avoiding direct sunlight. A plant that is 34 years old can produce 56 kg of dried leaves.
  • Tea: Traditionally, people have made tea from the leaves or chewed them directly. Gymnema sylvestre tea is commonly used to control appetite and support weight loss.
  • Extract: To extract gymnemic acid from the plant, one method involves crushing the plant material and soaking it in 3090% ethanol multiple times. The liquid is then filtered, and the ethanol is removed through ultrafiltration. Another method involves dissolving the extract in water, adjusting the pH with hydrochloric acid to precipitate gymnemic acid, and then stirring and drying it. The crude and raw gymnemic acid can be purified by recrystallizing it in a mixture of ethyl acetate and acetone.
  • Other Forms: Gymnema sylvestre is also available in powder, tablet, or capsule form. Tablets and capsules are useful for ensuring accurate dosing and avoiding the risk of taking too much.

Gymnema Sylvestre Dosage

The appropriate dosage of gymnema sylvestre varies based on multiple factors, such as age, overall health, and medical conditions to be treated. There is no universally established specified safe and effective dose. It is always advised to follow your doctors advice or the instructions on the product label. Gymnema sylvestre is generally considered safe when used as instructed.

  • Tea: Boil dried leaves of gymnema sylvestre for five minutes and steep for 15 minutes. The tea has a slightly bitter, earthy flavor. It is suggested that adding sweeteners may reduce its effectiveness.
  • Powder: Start with 2 grams (2,000 mg) and gradually increase to 4 grams (4,000 mg) as tolerated and suggested by the doctor.
  • Capsules or tablets: Take 100 mg three to four times daily.
  • For sugar-blocking effects: Take gymnema sylvestre supplements five to 10 minutes before consuming high-sugar foods.
  • Be cautious, as high doses of gymnema sylvestre can cause liver toxicity. There has been at least one report of drug-induced hepatitis in an adult with diabetes.

Who Should Avoid Gymnema Sylvestre?

Gymnema sylvestre is mostly considered safe when used in appropriate doses, but there are certain individuals who should avoid or use caution when taking it.

  • Pregnant and breastfeeding women: There is less research work done on the performance of Gymnema sylvestre while consumed by pregnant and breastfeeding women. Hence, it is advised for them to avoid the intake.
  • Children: It is mostly avoided to give gymnema sylvestre to children due to lack of proper research on the safety and appropriate dosage.
  • Individuals with low blood sugar (hypoglycemia): Gymnema sylvestre can be used to lower blood sugar levels. Individuals with a history of hypoglycemia or who are taking diabetes medications should consult with their doctors before using Gymnema sylvestre, as it may interact with their other medical treatments and further lower blood sugar, which could be dangerous for them.
  • Individuals with kidney or liver disease: Gymnema sylvestre is majorly metabolized by the liver and kidneys. People having impaired kidney or liver function may be at a higher risk of adverse effects and should avoid using it unless under the supervision of a healthcare provider.

Side Effects And Risks Of Using Gymnema Sylvestre

  • Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar): Gymnema sylvestre can possibly lower blood sugar levels, especially in individuals with diabetes or those taking diabetes medications. This can lead to hypoglycemic episodes, which can be dangerous if not properly managed.
  • Gastrointestinal issues: In some rare cases, people may experience mild gastrointestinal side effects, such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or diarrhea, when taking Gymnema sylvestre.
  • Allergic reactions: In rare cases, some people may observe allergic reactions, such as skin rashes, itching, or difficulty breathing, while consuming gymnema sylvestre.
  • Interactions with medications: Gymnema sylvestre may interact with certain medications, such as diabetes medications, blood thinners, and antidepressants. This can enhance or reduce the effectiveness of these medications or increase the risk of complications.
