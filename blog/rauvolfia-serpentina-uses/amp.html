Rauvolfia Serpentina – Uses, Dosage, Side Effects, Composition And More

What Is Rauvolfia Serpentina?

Rauvolfia serpentina is commonly known as Indian snakeroot, devil pepper, serpentine wood, sarpagandha or locally known as chandrika. It is a species of flower coming from the milkweed family Apocynaceae. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and East Asia, which is from India to Indonesia. It has been widely used in folk medicine in India for centuries to treat a wide variety of maladies, including snake and insect bites, febrile conditions, malaria, abdominal pain, and dysentery. It was also used as a uterine stimulant, febrifuge, and cure for insanity.

Rauvolfia Serpentina Chemical Constituents

Rauvolfia serpentina contains large quantities of alkaloids of the indole alkaloid family, including ajmaline, ajmalicine, reserpine, and serpentine, among others. Rauwolfia serpentina contains several other compounds, including carbohydrates, flavonoids, glycosides, phlobatannins, phenols, resins, saponins, and sterols. In addition to these, the dried roots of the plant have been found to contain new compounds such as indole alkaloids, iridoid glucosides, and sucrose derivatives.

Who Should Avoid Rauvolfia Serpentina?

Rauvolfia serpentina is a natural medicine, but it may not be suitable for everyone. While it has many benefits, there are certain people who should avoid it, and there can be side effects or risks if not used properly.

  • Pregnant or Breastfeeding Women: Due to a lack of proper research on the effects of this medicine on pregnant or breastfeeding women, it is generally not safe to use Rauvolfia serpentina because it may affect the babys health.
  • People with Low Blood Pressure: Since Rauvolfia serpentina is used to lower high blood pressure, people who already have low blood pressure should avoid it, as it could make their blood pressure drop too low, causing dizziness or fainting.
  • People Taking Certain Medications: If you are already taking medication for high blood pressure, heart problems, or mental health issues, you should avoid Rauvolfia serpentina unless advised by a doctor.
