How To Treat Fatty Liver With Homeopathy?

What is Fatty Liver Disease?

Fatty liver disease, also termed hepatic steatosis, involves the buildup of excess fat in the liver, a condition associated with alcohol consumption or other causes. Steatotic liver disease encompasses various conditions related to fat buildup in the liver, which ideally should contain only a small amount of fat.

When fat exceeds 5% of the liver’s weight, it becomes problematic. Treating fatty liver with homeopathy involves personalized remedies that aim to manage the root causes and symptoms associated with the condition.

Types of Fatty Liver

Fatty liver disease comprises distinct types based on the underlying causes and associated conditions. Understanding these classifications aids in proper diagnosis and targeted treatment approaches;

  • Alcohol-related liver disease (ALD): Excessive alcohol consumption contributes to ALD. As the liver filters alcohol, cells may die. Ordinarily, the liver replaces these cells, but with excessive alcohol intake, fat accumulation, known as steatosis, may occur.
  • Metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD): Formerly termed non-alcohol related fatty liver disease (NASLD), MASLD results from cardiometabolic risk factors rather than heavy alcohol use. Conditions such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, obesity, and lipid abnormalities contribute to this form of fatty liver disease. Even minimal alcohol intake alongside these risk factors can lead to MASLD.
  • Metabolic-associated steatohepatitis (MASH): MASH represents a severe progression of MASLD, wherein fat accumulation leads to liver inflammation, tissue damage, and scarring (fibrosis). It was previously referred to as non-alcohol related steatohepatitis (NASH).
  • MASLD and increased alcohol intake (MetALD): MetALD occurs when both metabolic risk elements and an overindulgence in alcohol lead to the accumulation of fat in the liver. People with cardiometabolic risk factors who consume alcohol in excess of defined limits per week fall into this category.
  • Other forms of Steatotic Liver Disease (SLD): Apart from alcohol use or cardiometabolic risk factors, SLD can arise due to medications, diseases, or unknown causes. Cases without a clear cause are labelled cryptogenic SLD.

Symptoms of Fatty Liver

Identifying symptoms of fatty liver disease can be challenging as the initial stages of alcohol-related liver disease (ALD) and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) often manifest without noticeable signs.

Nevertheless, specific individuals may encounter weariness or uneasiness in the upper right part of the abdomen, where the liver is situated. As the disease progresses to non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH) or cirrhosis, symptoms become more evident, indicating advanced liver damage:

  • Swollen Belly: An enlarged or distended abdomen could indicate fluid accumulation, a condition known as ascites, which occurs due to liver dysfunction in advanced stages of fatty liver disease.
  • Enlarged Blood Vessels: Visible enlarged blood vessels just beneath the skin’s surface might be present, particularly around the abdomen area. These veins can be a sign of increased pressure in the liver, a condition called portal hypertension.
  • Gynecomastia (Enlarged Breasts in Men): Men may notice an abnormal increase in breast size due to hormonal imbalances associated with advanced liver disease.
  • Red Palms: Palmar erythema, characterized by reddening of the palms, can indicate liver damage and is commonly observed in individuals with advanced liver conditions.
  • Jaundice: Yellowish discoloration of the skin and eyes, medically referred to as jaundice, occurs due to bilirubin accumulation in the body, indicating impaired liver function.
  • Gastrointestinal Symptoms: Nausea, unintended weight loss, or a loss of appetite may develop as liver function deteriorates. These symptoms often accompany advanced stages of fatty liver disease.
  • Fatigue and Mental Confusion: Persistent tiredness and mental confusion may arise, reflecting the body’s struggle with compromised liver function and the accumulation of toxins.

Causes Of Fatty Liver

The development of fatty liver disease (FLD) results from various underlying factors, encompassing both lifestyle and medical conditions. Understanding these causes helps in recognizing risk factors and addressing them to prevent or manage the condition effectively.

  • Alcohol Consumption: Excessive or frequent alcohol intake is a primary cause of alcoholic fatty liver disease. Prolonged and heavy alcohol consumption leads to the accumulation of fat in the liver, disrupting its normal function.
  • Cardiometabolic Risk Factors: Several conditions and risk factors linked to metabolic syndrome significantly contribute to the development of non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD). Individuals affected by these metabolic irregularities are more prone to FLD. These factors include:
    • Insulin resistance
    • Hypertension or high blood pressure
    • High cholesterol
    • High triglyceride levels
  • Medical Conditions: Several health conditions or irregularities in hormone levels make individuals more susceptible to fatty liver disease. The following ailments can contribute to the onset of this condition:
    • Obesity (characterized by high BMI)
    • Type 2 diabetes
    • Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
    • Obstructive sleep apnea
    • Hypothyroidism (low thyroid hormones)
    • Hypopituitarism (low pituitary gland hormones)
    • Hypogonadism (low sex hormones)

Diet And Lifestyle for Fatty Liver Management

While homeopathy may offer supportive measures, fundamental lifestyle adjustments are fundamental in both managing and preventing fatty liver disease. Implementing these health measures significantly contributes to enhancing liver health and combating the progression of this condition;

  • Weight Management: Shedding excess weight significantly benefits Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD). Striving for a Body Mass Index (BMI) within the 18.5 to 24.9 range or losing over 10% of body weight helps diminish liver fat and enhances Non-Alcoholic Steatohepatitis (NASH) if present.
  • Balanced Diet: Prioritize a well-rounded diet abundant in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and wholesome carbohydrates while moderating fat, sugar, and salt intake. Smaller, proportioned meals contribute to managing NAFLD. Opt for antioxidant-rich foods like berries, leafy greens, nuts, and healthy fats like olive oil, which support liver health.
  • Regular Exercise: Committing to 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercises weekly, such as brisk walking, cycling, or swimming, significantly aids in managing NAFLD. Even without weight loss, exercise enhances liver health, ameliorating fat accumulation.
  • Lifestyle Adjustments: Essential lifestyle modifications like quitting smoking, practicing stress-reducing techniques such as yoga or meditation, and ensuring adequate sleep significantly support liver health and overall well-being.
  • Alcohol Reduction: Although Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease isn’t alcohol-induced, limiting or abstaining from alcohol is advisable as it may exacerbate the condition. Restricting alcohol intake plays a crucial role in preventing and managing fatty liver disease.
  • Nutrition-Focused Lifestyle: Ensuring a balanced intake of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats via whole foods is key. This strategy not only provides adequate nutrition but also aids in weight management, a pivotal factor in liver health.
