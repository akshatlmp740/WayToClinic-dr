Garcinia Cambogia – Uses, Dosage, Side Effects, Composition And More

What is Garcinia Cambogia?

Garcinia Cambogia, now known as Garcinia gummi-gutta, is a tropical tree found in India and Southeast Asia. The fruit of this tree looks like a small pumpkin and comes in colors like green, yellow, or red. Names like red mango and Malabar tamarind also know it. The rind of this fruit contains hydroxycitric acid (HCA), which is used in dietary supplements.

It is popular to help with conditions like obesity and joint pain. People believe HCA can help with weight loss by preventing fat storage, controlling appetite, and boosting exercise endurance, though it’s not clear if it works in humans.

Garcinia Cambogia Chemical Constituents

Garcinia Cambogia is known for its potential weight loss benefits, primarily due to its chemical makeup. Let’s take a closer look at the key chemical constituents of this fruit;

Hydroxycitric Acid (HCA): The main active ingredient in Garcinia Cambogia is Hydroxycitric Acid (HCA). This substance makes up about 10% to 30% of the fruit’s rind. HCA is believed to aid in weight loss by inhibiting an enzyme called ATP-citrate lyase. This enzyme plays a role in converting excess carbohydrates into fat. By blocking this enzyme, HCA may help reduce fat storage in the body.

Forms and Sources of HCA: HCA can be found in several forms, including calcium, magnesium, and potassium salts. These salts are often used in dietary supplements. Besides being present in the fruit, HCA can also be produced by certain bacteria. Additionally, it can be synthesized from citric acid through a chemical process involving dehydration and oxidation.

Other Constituents: Garcinia Cambogia also contains other nutrients and compounds. Its seeds are rich in fats, and the fruit itself has vitamins and minerals like sodium, potassium, calcium, and iron. These components contribute to the fruit’s nutritional value and potential health benefits.

Garcinia Cambogia Preparation

Garcinia Cambogia is popular for its potential weight loss benefits, mainly due to the presence of hydroxycitric acid (HCA). Extracting and purifying HCA from Garcinia Cambogia involves a detailed process. Here is a simple, step-by-step guide to preparing Garcinia Cambogia extract;

Step 1: Extracting Garcinia Cambogia

First, you need to gather Garcinia Cambogia fruit. The fruit is then crushed to create a pulp. This pulp is mixed with water and heated to help release the hydroxycitric acid (HCA) from the fruit.

Step 2: Neutralizing the Extract Solution

Next, the extract solution from the first step is neutralized. This is done by adding a base, such as calcium hydroxide, which helps to form hydroxy calcium citrate.

Step 3: Washing and Removing Salt

The hydroxy calcium citrate is then washed to remove any impurities or salt. This step ensures the purity of the extract.

Step 4: Acid Hydrolysis and Decolorizing

After washing, the hydroxy calcium citrate undergoes acid hydrolysis. This process breaks down the compound further and removes any unwanted colors. The solution is then concentrated by heating.

Step 5: Refining and Crystallizing HCA

The concentrated solution is refined and left to cool, allowing the hydroxycitric acid to crystallize. This step helps to separate pure HCA crystals from the solution.

Step 6: Centrifuging, Drying, and Crushing

Finally, the crystal substance is centrifuged to separate any remaining liquid. The crystals are then dried, crushed into a fine powder, and mixed to ensure uniformity.

Garcinia Cambogia Dosage

Garcinia Cambogia is a popular herbal supplement often used for weight loss. The dosage of Garcinia Cambogia can vary based on individual factors like age and health conditions.

Garcinia Cambogia Dosage for Adults

For adults, Garcinia Cambogia supplements typically contain hydroxycitric acid (HCA), the active ingredient. The recommended dosage is usually 500 mg, taken three times a day, 30 to 60 minutes before meals. Some studies suggest a range of 900 to 1500 mg of HCA per day.

It’s essential to choose a reputable brand that contains 50-60% HCA and follow the instructions on the label. It’s crucial to follow the recommended guidelines and consult a healthcare professional before use.

Garcinia Cambogia Dosage for Children

There is limited information and research on the appropriate dosage of Garcinia Cambogia for children. Due to the lack of scientific data, it is best to avoid giving this supplement to children without consulting a healthcare professional. Always prioritize safety and seek medical advice before introducing any new supplement to a child’s diet.

Who should avoid Garcinia Cambogia?

Garcinia Cambogia, a popular supplement for weight loss, may not be suitable for everyone.

  • Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid it due to the lack of reliable information on its safety for these groups.
  • People with bipolar disorder should also steer clear, as Garcinia can worsen mania.
  • Individuals with liver disease should not take Garcinia Cambogia since it might harm the liver.
  • Those scheduled for surgery should stop taking the supplement at least two weeks prior, as it can affect blood sugar levels and slow blood clotting.
  • Additionally, people with dementia or Alzheimer’s disease should avoid Garcinia Cambogia because it can interfere with certain medications.

Side Effects and Risks of using Garcinia Cambogia

While Garcinia Cambogia is popular for weight loss, it comes with several side effects and risks. Common Side Effects:

Common side effects: The most common side effects of using Garcinia Cambogia include;

  • Nausea
  • Stomach discomfort
  • Headache

These symptoms can vary in intensity and may affect some users more than others.

Serious Health Risks: Some people have reported serious liver problems after taking products containing Garcinia Cambogia, although it is unclear if Garcinia is the direct cause.

  • There have been instances of liver damage, jaundice, seizures, and cardiovascular disorders.
  • It may also cause a severe condition called rhabdomyolysis in users of some products that contain Garcinia.

Interactions with Medications: Garcinia Cambogia may interact with several medications.

  • It can lower blood sugar levels, posing a risk for people taking diabetes medications
  • It also slows blood clotting, which can increase the risk of bleeding if taken with anticoagulant or antiplatelet drugs
  • Additionally, Garcinia can increase serotonin levels in the brain, which might lead to dangerous interactions with serotonergic drugs, potentially causing heart problems, seizures, and vomiting
  • Furthermore, it can increase the risk of liver damage when taken with other hepatotoxic drugs
