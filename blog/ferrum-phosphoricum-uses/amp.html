Ferrum Phosphoricum Chemical Constituents

Ferrum phosphoricum is a homeopathic medicine made of iron and phosphorus. The chemical constituents of ferrum phosphoricum consist of Iron (Fe³⁺), which is the primary metal ion in it, providing the iron component of the compound. The phosphate ion (PO₄³⁻) is the phosphate part of the compound, which further combines with the iron ion to form iron phosphate.

The chemical formula for Ferrum phosphoricum is represented as FePO₄. It includes one iron ion and one phosphate ion bonded together. This compound can exist in different forms, including anhydrous (without water) or hydrated (with water molecules attached) versions.

Ferrum Phosphoricum Preparation

Ferrum phosphoricum or iron phosphate is prepared through the method of trituration and dilution. Iron phosphate is prepared by dissolving 10 parts of a 1% iron chloride solution, 10 parts of a 1% sodium phosphate solution, and 20 parts of water. This mixture further forms a solid substance that is washed with water and later reacted with silver nitrate.

The careful process of preparation depends on the inclined results expected.

  • Trituration: The process of preparation starts with the raw material, which is iron phosphate (FePO₄). It is further grounded into a fine powder. Then, this powder is mixed with lactose in a mortar and pestle. The resultant mixture is then triturated for around an hour to make sure that iron phosphate is thoroughly mixed with the lactose. This particular process is repeated to create an initial homeopathic potency of about 3X or 6X.
  • Dilution: After trituration, the mixture is diluted in a liquid, which is alcohol or water. Also, a small amount of triturated powder is mixed with a liquid base and shaken vigorously (a process known as succussion). This dilution and succussion process is repeated multiple times to get 12X, 30X, or higher potencies.
