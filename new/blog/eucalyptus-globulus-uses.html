Eucalyptus Globulus – Uses, Dosage, Side Effects, Composition And More

By Dheeraj Sharma August 30, 2024 Medicine

What Is Eucalyptus Globulus?

Eucalyptus globulus is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is also commonly known as southern blue gum or blue gum. Eucalyptus globulus is a tall and evergreen tree only found in southeastern Australia.

This species has smooth bark, juvenile leaves that are whitish and waxy on the lower surface of the tree, glossy green, lance-shaped adult leaves, glaucous, ribbed flower buds arranged singly or in groups of three or seven in leaf axils, white flowers, and woody fruit.

Eucalyptus globulus generally grows to a height of 45 m (148 ft) or sometimes could be a stunted shrub. The essential oils found in the leaves of the eucalyptus globulus are a powerful antiseptic and are used for treating coughs and colds, sore throats, and other infections.

Eucalyptus Globulus Chemical Constituents

Eucalyptus globulus contains quinic, dihydroxyphenylacetic and caffeic acids, bis(hexahydroxydiphenoyl (HHDP))-glucose, galloyl-bis(HHDP)-glucose, galloyl-HHDP-glucose, isorhamentin-hexoside, quercetin-hexoside, methylellagic acid (EA)-pentose conjugate, myricetin-rhamnoside, isorhamnetin-rhamnoside, mearnsetin, phloridzin, mearnsetin-hexoside, luteolin and a proanthocyanidin B-type dimer, digalloylglucose and catechin.

It also consists of oxygenated monoterpenes, which have 63.9% of the oil, with 1,8-cineole as the significant component of it. Monoterpene hydrocarbons includes 35.3% of the oil, with p-cimene, α-pinene, α-limonene, γ-terpinene, β-pinene, and β-myrcene as the dominant components in eucalyptus globulus along with monoterpene epoxides with 63.1% of the oil.

Eucalyptus Globulus Preparation

Eucalyptus globulus has significant medical uses. Hence, it is prepared through various methods for a better yield. Let us learn in detail about the preparation of eucalyptus globulus.

  • Process of Extracts Drying: By this process eucalyptus globulus 500 g leaves are dried in shade for a week and after removing all the moisture, which is enough to get entirely mixed with extracts. The leaves are grinded up to form powder. Dissolve a mixture of this powder in different concentrations (0, 10, 30, 50,70 plus and) v/v % in ethanol. Allow this blend to rest in a sonic bath at room temperature for 3 hours. Filter the mixture with a 0.2 μm syringe filter and analyze for analysis.
  • Extracting Essential Oil: To extract the essential oil, chop up the leaves and stems of an Eucalyptus plant and put them in a distillation machine. This makes the oil, as well as other products va,porize and go into a condenser, where the substances present start to form from vapor in liquid. The vapor is cooled back to liquid by a condenser in which you can harvest the water collected.
  • Extracting Essential Oil Components: In this method, the oil to be tinctured beforehand is removed from the eucalyptus plant (in the form of essential oil), and it extracts its water or a solution of organic solvent. You can repeat this with the solution or pure organic solvent. This technique is helpful for the preparation of compounds Macrocarpal A, Macrocarpal B, and macroplamin C from Eucalyptus.

Benefits Of Eucalyptus Globulus

Eucalyptus globulus is a versatile and valuable tree with a range of benefits, from its medicinal properties to its environmental and industrial applications. Here are some of them discussed below:

  • Medicinal Properties: Eucalyptus globulus leaves are rich in essential oils containing compounds like 1,8-cineole (eucalyptol) that exhibit anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial activity. Eucalyptus oil is used in traditional and modern medicine for the relief of respiratory symptoms, such as nasal stuffiness, cough, and sinus pigmentosa. It can be taken in different forms, such as inhalation, topical or oral. Research has also been done on the oil to evaluate its ability to relieve pain, reduce fevers, and even aid in wound healing.
  • Aromatic Benefits: Eucalyptus globulus occurs widely with a smell that is refreshing but unique, and it finds various applications, as in aromatherapy, where the oil is used in diffusers and candles to make one feel more relaxed and happy. Eucalyptus globulus can be used in personal care items, such as soaps and shampoos because it smells nice, yet it is therapeutic.
  • Air Purification: Eucalyptus globulus is known to reduce the pollution of VOCs, Formaldehyde, and Carbon dioxide from air. With its large leaves and fast-growing habit, the tree is able to improve both indoor as well outdoor air quality efficiently.
  • Insect Repellent: Park owners use fragrant eucalyptus from the eucalyptus globulus tree, whose powerfully aromatic oils also offer some repellent effect. It makes the tree valuable in disease-prone insect areas since it can help keep these pests away naturally.
  • Timber Production: Eucalyptus globulus (Tasmanian Blue Gum) is the most important commercially grown species. It has numerous applications for its standard timber, including furniture and cabinetry, construction materials, and pulpwood for paper production.

Who Should Avoid Eucalyptus Globulus?

Eucalyptus globulus is used as a medicine in various sectors. But it should be taken under the prescription of a specialist doctor. Proper dosage and application guidelines should be followed to minimize the risks and ensure safe use. Here are the details on who should avoid using eucalyptus globulus:

  • Children: Eucalyptus globulus essential oil and extracts are avoided for children as their bodies are susceptible as compared to adults. Ingestion of eucalyptus globulus essential oil is toxic and could prove to lead to specific side effects.
  • Pregnant and Breastfeeding Women: The use of eucalyptus globulus through pregnancy and breastfeeding is not well supported due to possible risks to the developing fetus or nursing infant.
  • Individuals with Seizure Disorders: Eucalyptus globulus consists of compounds that may lower the seizure threshold, making it unsuitable for those with seizure disorders or epilepsy.
  • People with Liver or Kidney Conditions: Eucalyptus globulus and its compounds can be metabolized by the liver and kidneys, and their use may create complications for individuals with pre-existing liver or kidney problems.

Potential Side Effects And Risks Of Eucalyptus Globulus:

  • Skin Irritation and Allergic Reactions: Topical application of eucalyptus globulus essential oil can cause skin irritation, redness, and allergic reactions, especially in sensitive individuals.
  • Gastrointestinal Issues: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain could be caused after the use of eucalyptus globulus in certain rare cases.
  • Neurotoxicity: High doses of eucalyptus globulus in some individuals may result in neurotoxic effects, leading to symptoms like dizziness, headaches, and seizures.
  • Respiratory Irritation: After the consumption of eucalyptus globulus essential oil, the vapors can irritate the respiratory tract, potentially exacerbating conditions like asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
