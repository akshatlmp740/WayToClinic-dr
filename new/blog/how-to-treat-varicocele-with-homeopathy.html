What is Varicocele?

Varicocele is an affection that consists of the hypertrophy and torsion of the veins located in the scrotum, similar to varicose veins that can affect the legs. These veins, referred to as pampiniform plexus, are very important in the process of detaching blood from the testicles. They hold blood in veins and when these veins become dilated, then there is poor blood circulation, such discomforts or other related complications may occur.

In some men, varicocele is present on the left side of the male genitalia, mainly due to several physical characteristics of the male reproductive system, where the condition affects at least 1525-year-old men. Despite the many silent cases, some men may suffer from mild pain, a swollen feeling in the scrotum, or the sensation of it being heavy. Varicocele also becomes one of the biggest factors of male infertility because it can also affect the quality of sperm produced.

What are the Symptoms of Varicocele?

The symptoms of varicocele can differ from person to person. Some people might not notice any signs, while others may feel discomfort or see visible changes. Here are the common symptoms explained for varicocele:

  • Infertility Problems: It may be harder to conceive because varicocele can lower sperm count, reduce sperm movement, and affect overall quality. Hormonal changes caused by varicocele can also affect fertility.
  • Testicle Shrinkage: The testicle on the affected side may become smaller due to poor blood flow and pressure in the veins.
  • Pain or Discomfort: A chronic pain or discomfort in the scrotum, which becomes worse when standing for some time, during any physical activity, or towards the evening and sleeps off when lying down.

Causes of a Varicocele

Varicocele occurs when the veins inside the scrotum, known as the pampiniform plexus, become enlarged or twisted. This happens due to problems with blood flow in the area.

  • Valve Malfunction in the Veins: Veins in the scrotum have valves that help blood flow in the right direction toward the heart. When these valves dont work properly, blood can pool in the veins, causing them to stretch and enlarge.
  • Poor Blood Circulation: The scrotal veins also open into larger veins in the abdominal cavity. When there is pressure inside these veins, then it becomes difficult for blood to drain outside of the scrotum and is known as varicocele.
  • Hormonal Imbalances: Testosterone and other androgen levels are also suspected of producing or exacerbating varicocele, which may be associated with hormonal alterations.
  • Age and Development: Varicocele most commonly develops during the teenage years when the blood supply to the developing testicles is increased.
  • Lifestyle Factors: Physical exertion, standing for a long time, or lifting very heavy objects may occasionally raise pressure within the veins and cause a varicocele.
