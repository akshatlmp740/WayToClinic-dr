Swertia Chirata – Uses, Dosage, Side Effects, Composition And More

Swertia Chirata – Uses, Dosage, Side Effects, Composition And More

By Dheeraj Sharma October 27, 2024 Medicine

What is Swertia Chirata?

Chirata is the common name of Swertia chirata, a medicinal plant of the temperate zone of the Himalayas. It is generally referred to by its bitter properties and is used in both Ayurvedic and Unani systems of medicine. The plant is known to have diverse health benefits, including being used as a tonic that helps with digestion, appetite, and liver health.

The plant Swertia chirata also cures ailments like fever, malaria, skin disease, and digestive disorders. That is why its active ingredients have been known to display anti-inflammatory and antibacterial attributes as well as hypoglycemic effects important for tackling diabetes and many other disorders. Owing to these therapeutic uses, the plant Swertia chirata is widely used in herbal formulations and products.

Swertia Chirata Chemical Constituents

Swertia chirata, commonly known as Chirata, contains a variety of chemical constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties. Key components include:

  • Bitter Principles:
    • Amarogentin: The most bitter compound known, contributing to its digestive benefits.
    • Swertiamarin: Known for its antidiabetic and hepatoprotective effects.
    • Swerchirin: Another bitter compound that supports liver health.
  • Alkaloids: Various alkaloids are present, which may exhibit anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.
  • Flavonoids: These compounds provide antioxidant benefits and may help reduce inflammation.
  • Xanthones: Xanthones like mangiferin are noted for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory effects.
  • Triterpenoids: Compounds such as oleanolic acid and ursolic acid have anti-inflammatory and hepatoprotective properties.
  • Glycosides: Including sweroside and amaroswerin, which contribute to the herb’s therapeutic effects.
  • Other Compounds: The plant also contains tannins, saponins, and phenolic compounds that enhance its medicinal value.

Swertia Chirata Preparation

Swertia chirata, commonly known as Chirata, is prepared for medicinal use through various methods that extract its beneficial compounds. Here are some common preparation methods:

  • Decoction: The whole plant or its parts (leaves and stems) are boiled in water. Usually, 30g of the dried herb is in one liter of water, which is boiled and then simmered until it is halved in volume. This decoction can be administered orally. The preferred measure is 15- 30 ml (1 to 2 tablespoons).
  • Infusion: Infusion is made by soaking the dried leaves of the plant in hot water, which may be accompanied by flavors such as cloves or cinnamon. This method isolates the bitter principles as well as other active constituents of the plant.
  • Powdered Form: Another possible usage is grinding the dried raw plant material into a very fine powder, which is contained in capsules or drunk with water. This form is often usable as a dietary additive for other meals and foods that you consume on a daily basis.
  • Tincture: An infusion can be made by soaking the dried herb in alcohol or glycerin for two to four weeks, depending on the active compound you want to extract. The final products are best consumed in small quantities at a time, preferably one time a day with a full glass of water.
  • Topical Application: For skin afflictions, the herb can be prepared in powdered form and, when mixed with water, spread directly on the affected part of the body.

Swertia Chirata Mother Tincture

Swertia Chirata mother tincture is a homeopathic medicine prepared from the plant Swertia chirata, which has many uses. This tincture is really good for curing a number of diseases, such as digestive disorders, fever, and liver-related diseases. It serves as an appetizer and a laxative, and it can be used to help people with anorexia or anyone who has a poor appetite.

Moreover, it is used to treat respiratory diseases like cough, bronchitis, tuberculosis, and asthma. This depends on the recommendation, where the common dosage is about 10 drops diluted in half a cup of water three times daily. It is relatively safe. However, pregnant or breastfeeding women should seek the opinion of a doctor before taking the supplements.

Benefits of Swertia Chirata

Swertia Chirata offers a wide range of health benefits. Some of the key benefits include:

  • Digestive Health: Specifically, Swertia chirata is well sought out for its functionalities in curing dyspepsia and conditions of loss of appetite.
  • Liver Support: It has a hepatoprotective effect and improves liver function and detoxification if there is a liver problem, which is very common nowadays.
  • Antimalarial Properties: The herb is well known for managing fevers caused by malaria, terminating the plant’s function in curing this disease.
  • Blood Sugar Regulation: Swertia chirata contains hypoglycaemic activity and can be used to treat diabetes by controlling the blood sugar level or simply stimulating insulin release.
  • Anti-inflammatory Effects: The plant exhibits anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate symptoms associated with various inflammatory conditions.
  • Antimicrobial Activity: Swertia chirata has demonstrated antibacterial and antifungal properties, making it effective against various pathogens.
  • Skin Health: It is used in treating skin diseases, including eczema and acne, due to its anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
  • Antiparasitic Effects: The herb is effective against intestinal worms, acting as an anthelmintic to help eliminate these parasites from the body.
  • Cardiovascular Benefits: Swertia chirata may help in reducing blood pressure and improving heart health due to its cardioprotective properties.
  • Mental Health Support: Traditionally used to address conditions like melancholia and anxiety, it may have a calming effect on the nervous system.

What Does Swertia Chirata Treat?

Here are some of the common ailments Swertia Chirata treats:

  • Liver Disorders: Swertia Chirata is useful in diseases of the liver such as jaundice, hepatitis or poor liver function. It helps detoxify the liver and supports bile secretion.
  • Fever: Because of this, it is commonly taken to treat fevers, especially intermittent ones such as malaria fever. The plant used in the treatment of Chirata has antipyretic effects and is capable of managing high body temperature.
  • Digestive Issues: Swertia Chirata is used for conditions such as indigestion, constipation, bloating, and loss of appetite because it boosts peristaltic movements.
  • Skin Problems: It might help treat skin disorders such as acne, skin rash, and infection since it possesses anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties.
  • Diabetes: Swertia Chirata is quite effective in regulating blood sugar levels, and therefore, it has potential use in preparing antidiabetic products that assist in glucose utilization.
  • Respiratory Conditions: It also proves useful in treating bronchitis, asthma, and all kinds of respiratory infections caused by inflammation and microbes.
  • Blood Purification: Swertia Chirata is effective for cleaning the blood, thus acting as a blood purifier that improves the body’s system.

Swertia Chirata Dosage

Swertia chirata, commonly used in various forms, has specific dosage recommendations depending on the preparation. Here are the typical dosages:

  • Chirata Powder: 1-3 grams taken twice a day or as directed by a healthcare professional.
  • Chirata Tablets: One tablet is taken twice a day or as advised by a physician.
  • Chirata Capsules: One capsule is taken once daily or as directed by a healthcare provider.
  • Chirata Decoction (Kwath): Prepare by boiling 1 tablespoon of dried Chirata powder in 2 cups of water until reduced to half. Drink 3-4 teaspoons of this decoction twice daily.
  • Mother Tincture: For Swertia Chirata mother tincture, the usual dosage is 10 drops diluted in half a cup of water, taken three times a day or as directed.

Swertia Chirata Dosage For Adults

Adults are advised different doses of Swertia chirata depending on the prepared form of the herb. Even so, as for Chirata powder, the preferable doses are in the range of 1 to 3 grams per day, twice a day, or as advised by a doctor. The generic dosage of Chirata tablets is 1 tablet two times a day.

For Chirata capsules, the recommended dosage is one capsule per day or as directed by a qualified practitioner. Also, to prepare a decoction (with), one can reduce 1 tablespoon of the dried Chirata powder in 2 cups of boiling water to half and then take 3-4 teaspoons twice daily. Regarding those using Swertia Chirata mother tincture, the dosage is 10 drops in half a cup of water 3 times a day.

Swertia Chirata Dosage For Children

Swertia chirata should be administered with precaution under the supervision of a pediatrician. In most cases, the suggested dosage is lower than what is seen in adult patients. Chirata powder should be taken about 0.5 to 1 gm once or twice daily, depending upon the child’s age and health. Where it is used in the form of Chirata tablets, a half tablet is recommended once or twice a day; where it is in Chirata capsules form, one capsule is recommended daily.

When preparing a decoction, one boils one teaspoon of dried Chirata powder in one cup of water until it becomes half; later, one takes 1-2 teaspoons of the decoction two times each day. The dosage recommendation for mother tincture is five drops in half a cup of water thrice a day.

Who Should Avoid Swertia Chirata?

  • Pregnant Women: There is a lack of sufficient data on the safety of use during pregnancy; pregnant women should consult a doctor before trying it.
  • Breastfeeding mothers: Uncertain risk information; it is best not to use or consult a doctor.
  • Individuals with Diabetes: May cause hypoglycemia when taken with antidiabetic drugs; requires blood sugar monitoring on a constant basis.
  • Patients with Intestinal Ulcers: This medication may exacerbate peptic or duodenal ulcers, so it should not be taken if one experiences one or both.
  • People Scheduled for Surgery: Stop using at least two weeks before surgery because of possible effects on blood sugar management during and following surgery.
  • Individuals with Heart Disease: Lack of adequate safety information; please consult your doctor before using it.

What Are The Side Effects And Risks Of Using Swertia Chirata?

  • Gastrointestinal Issues: It may cause nausea, vomiting, and upset stomach because it has a bitter taste.
  • Dizziness and Numbness: Some users have described other side effects, including feelings of dizziness and numbness in the hands.
  • Hypoglycemia: Low blood sugar, particularly when the medication is used in combination with others for diabetes.
  • Allergic Reactions: While it is unlikely that patients will have any adverse reactions to the treatment, it is suggested that any reactions that are totally out of the ordinary should be reported.
  • Interference with Blood Sugar Control: It may increase perioperative blood glucose and, hence, requires careful handling.
