Nux Vomica – Uses, Dosage, Side Effects, Composition And More

By Deeksha Katiyar April 5, 2024 Medicine

What is Nux Vomica?

Nux vomica originates from a tree and is primarily sourced from its seeds, which are utilized for medicinal purposes. Despite its historical use for a range of ailments, scientific evidence supporting its efficacy remains lacking. This plant is associated with significant safety concerns due to its composition of toxic chemicals such as strychnine and brucine.

Nux Vomica Chemical Constituents

Nux vomica has a variety of chemical constituents that are known for their potent effects on the human body. Among these constituents, strychnine and brucine stand out as primary toxic alkaloids, comprising a significant portion of nux vomica’s composition, typically ranging from 1.8% to 5.3%. Alongside strychnine and brucine, nux vomica contains a variety of minor alkaloids, including;

Main Constituents

  • Strychnine
  • Brucine

Minor Alkaloids

  • Chlorogenic acid
  • Glycoside
  • Isostrychnine
  • N-oxystrychnine
  • Protostrychnine
  • Pseudostrychnine
  • Vomicine

Other Substances

  • Brucine
  • Iridoids
  • Loganic acid
  • Loganin

Nux Vomica Preparation

To prepare nux vomica, a method involving several sequential steps is followed:

  1. Crushing and Sieving: Nux vomica is initially crushed and sieved using a mesh sieve ranging from 10 to 40, resulting in the formation of coarse powder.
  2. Heating Reflux Extraction: A portion of the coarse powder, typically weighing between 100g and 200g, is subjected to heating reflux extraction with an alcohol acid solution, usually ranging from 1000 mL to 2000 mL. Following extraction, centrifugation is performed to remove precipitates, and the alcohol acid solution is recovered under reduced pressure to yield a concentrate.
  3. pH Adjustment and Solvent Extraction: The concentrate undergoes pH adjustment using a suitable agent to attain a pH value ranging from 9 to 14. Subsequently, the adjusted concentrate is subjected to extraction with an extraction solvent, typically ranging from 3000mL to 4000mL. The recovered solvent is then evaporated under reduced pressure, and the resultant product is dried to obtain nux vomica total alkaloids powder.
  4. Supernatant Isolation: The nux vomica total alkaloids powder is dissolved in a mixed acid solution and then centrifuged to obtain the supernatant.
  5. Further pH Adjustment and Solvent Extraction: Using a pH adjusting agent, the supernatant’s pH value is adjusted to 10 to 13. This adjusted supernatant is then extracted with dichloromethane or ethyl acetate. After solvent recovery under reduced pressure and subsequent drying, nux vomica total alkaloids are obtained.

Nux Vomica Dosage

The selection of Nux vomica potency and dosage depends on individual case presentation, with higher potencies reserved for cases where symptoms closely align with the medicine. It’s essential to follow healthcare provider guidance and monitor symptoms closely during treatment. Here are some dosage recommendations for nux vomica for both adults and kids;

  • The recommended dosage for adults typically ranges from 5 to 10 drops orally, once daily, or as directed by a healthcare professional.

Who Should Avoid Nux Vomica?

Nux vomica contains two active ingredients, strychnine, and brucine, which are known to be toxic. One should always seek medical advice before taking it.

What are the Side Effects and Risks of Using Nux Vomica?

Also, individuals should inform their treating doctor about their medical issues, as it is not safe to be used in some instances and may also cause side effects that are listed below:

Avoidance Recommendations

  • Individuals with liver disease should avoid Nux vomica due to the risk of liver damage associated with its use.
  • High doses of Nux vomica or prolonged treatment should be avoided as they can lead to severe symptoms such as restlessness, anxiety, dizziness, back stiffness, liver failure, breathing problems, and seizures.

Risk Factors and Side Effects

Nux vomica, containing strychnine, is classified as unsafe due to its poisonous nature. Ingesting even small amounts can lead to fatal consequences. Here are some of its potential side effects;

  • Neurological side effects are severe and include restlessness, anxiety, dizziness, muscle stiffness, convulsions, and breathing difficulties.
  • The plant-based supplement may interact with certain medications, especially antipsychotics, leading to dangerous drug interactions.
  • Common side effects of Nux vomica include nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and heart palpitations.
  • Overdosing on Nux vomica can result in severe adverse effects on the liver and kidneys, emphasizing the importance of adhering to recommended dosage instructions.

Caution should be exercised when considering the use of Nux vomica, especially in individuals with liver disease or when high doses are involved. It’s crucial to consult a healthcare professional before using Nux vomica, particularly for the treatment of male infertility or erectile dysfunction, to assess potential risks and ensure safe usage.
