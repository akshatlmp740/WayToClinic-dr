Berberis Vulgaris – Uses, Dosage, Side Effects, Composition And More

By Deeksha Katiyar February 28, 2024 Medicine

What is Berberis Vulgaris?

Berberis Vulgaris, commonly known as European barberry, is a shrub native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its medicinal properties. The plant contains a variety of alkaloids, including berberine, which is believed to be responsible for its therapeutic effects. Berberis Vulgaris is commonly used to treat various health conditions, including digestive problems, urinary tract infections, and skin conditions.

Berberis Vulgaris chemical constituents

  • Berberine: A major alkaloid found in Berberis Vulgaris, berberine has antibacterial, antiviral, and anti-inflammatory properties. It is effective against a wide range of bacteria, including E. coli, Salmonella, and Staphylococcus aureus. Berberine has also been shown to inhibit the growth of some viruses, including influenza and herpes simplex virus.
  • Oxyberberine: Another alkaloid found in Berberis Vulgaris, oxyberberine has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It has been shown to protect cells from damage caused by free radicals and has been shown to reduce inflammation in animal models of arthritis and other inflammatory diseases.
  • Palmatine: A third alkaloid found in Berberis Vulgaris, palmatine has antibacterial and antifungal properties. It is effective against a wide range of bacteria, including E. coli, Salmonella, and Staphylococcus aureus.
  • Berberine hydrochloride: A water-soluble derivative of berberine, berberine hydrochloride treats diarrhoea and other gastrointestinal problems. It effectively reduces the frequency and severity of diarrhoea in adults and children. Berberine hydrochloride also treats other gastrointestinal problems, like irritable bowel syndrome and Crohn’s disease.
  • Berberine sulphate: Another water-soluble derivative of berberine, berberine sulphate is used to treat urinary tract infections and other bacterial infections. It effectively treats urinary tract infections caused by E. coli and other bacteria. Berberine sulphate is also used to treat other bacterial infections, like skin infections and respiratory infections.

Berberis Vulgaris Preparation

Berberis Vulgaris can be prepared differently, each with its benefits and applications. Here are some of the most common methods of preparation:

  • Tincture:
    • A tincture is made by soaking the dried bark of Berberis Vulgaris in alcohol, typically vodka or grain alcohol.
    • The mixture is left to steep for several weeks, allowing the alcohol to extract the active compounds from the bark.
    • The resulting tincture is a concentrated liquid extract that can be taken orally or applied topically.
  • Tea:
    • Tea can be made by steeping the dried leaves or bark of Berberis Vulgaris in hot water.
    • The leaves and bark contain various beneficial compounds, including berberine, oxy-berberine, and palmatine.
    • Berberis Vulgaris tea tastes slightly bitter and can be enjoyed hot or cold.
  • Capsules:
    • Capsules containing Berberis Vulgaris extract are available commercially.
    • The extract is typically standardised to contain a specific amount of berberine, the primary active compound in Berberis Vulgaris.
  • Ointment:
    • An ointment can be prepared by mixing Berberis Vulgaris extract with a base such as petroleum jelly or beeswax.
    • The ointment can be applied topically to treat various skin conditions, including eczema, psoriasis, and acne.
  • Other Methods of Preparation:
    • Berberis Vulgaris can also be used to make syrup, a glycerite, or a fluid extract.
    • These preparations are all made using different extraction methods and have slightly different properties.

When choosing a Berberis Vulgaris preparation, it is important to consider the desired use and the individual’s health status.

Berberis Vulgaris Mother Tincture

Berberis Vulgaris mother tincture is a concentrated form of the herb that is used to make homoeopathic remedies. The mother tincture is made by soaking the fresh leaves of Berberis Vulgaris in alcohol. The tincture is then diluted and potentised, shaking vigorously and diluting with water or alcohol. The resulting solution is used to make homoeopathic remedies.

  • Antiviral: Berberis Vulgaris has also been effective against various viruses, including DNA and RNA viruses. Studies have shown that Berberis Vulgaris extract can inhibit the replication of viruses such as herpes simplex virus, influenza virus, respiratory syncytial virus, and hepatitis B virus.

Who should avoid Berberis Vulgaris? / Side effects and risks of using Berberis Vulgaris

Berberis Vulgaris is generally considered safe for most people when used in moderate doses for a short time. However, some people may experience side effects, such as:

  • Nausea: Berberis Vulgaris may cause nausea, resulting in feelings of sickness and an inclination to vomit.
  • Vomiting: Berberis Vulgaris may induce vomiting, leading to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents.
  • Diarrhoea: Berberis Vulgaris may cause diarrhoea, resulting in frequent, loose, or watery stools.
  • Stomach pain: Berberis Vulgaris may cause stomach pain, leading to abdominal discomfort or pain.
  • Headache: Berberis Vulgaris may trigger headaches, causing pain or discomfort in the head.
  • Dizziness: Berberis Vulgaris may induce dizziness, resulting in a sensation of spinning or lightheadedness.
  • Rash: Berberis Vulgaris may cause skin rashes, leading to red, itchy, or inflamed areas on the skin.
  • Itching: Berberis Vulgaris may cause itching, resulting in an uncomfortable sensation that provokes the urge to scratch.
  • Increased heart rate: Berberis Vulgaris may increase the heart rate, leading to a faster heartbeat.
  • Lowered blood pressure: Berberis Vulgaris may lower blood pressure, resulting in decreased force exerted by blood against artery walls.
  • Berberis Vulgaris may also interact with some medications, including:
    • Blood thinners (such as warfarin and heparin)
    • Antibiotics (such as ciprofloxacin and erythromycin)
    • Diabetes medications (such as metformin and sulfonylureas)
  • People who are pregnant or breastfeeding should avoid using Berberis Vulgaris, as it may cause uterine contractions and harm the developing foetus or newborn baby.
  • People with the following conditions should also avoid using Berberis Vulgaris:
    • Liver disease: Individuals with liver disease should avoid Berberis Vulgaris, as it may worsen liver function and cause further damage.
    • Kidney disease: Berberis Vulgaris may harm individuals with kidney disease, potentially leading to impaired kidney function and worsening of the condition.
    • Heart disease: People with heart disease should avoid Berberis Vulgaris, as it may interfere with heart medications and potentially worsen heart conditions.
    • High blood pressure: Berberis Vulgaris may interact with blood pressure medications, potentially causing dangerously low blood pressure in individuals with hypertension.
    • Diabetes: Berberis Vulgaris may interfere with diabetes medications, potentially leading to unstable blood sugar levels and complications in individuals with diabetes.
    • Stomach ulcers: Berberis Vulgaris may irritate stomach ulcers, causing increased pain, bleeding, and discomfort in individuals with this condition.

If you are considering using Berberis Vulgaris, it is important to talk to your healthcare practitioner first to discuss the potential risks and benefits.
